Project 3 - Robot Factory II

Type: Game

Concepts: Scrolling tile-map, Finite-State-Machine, perspectives

Description:

The premise of this game is that the player can move around a static tile-map larger than the view of the player. This means that the tile-map is still static just as in the previous project, but the player can only see a small portion of it in any given frame. To view another part of the map, the player must move to it. The robots, player, and walls are pre-defined, but the discs are generated at random. The concept of a game state was also applied to this project, to understand the incorporation of more complicated actions.

Gameplay:

To begin the game, click the starting screen. Move the black robot around the map, noting that the perspective moves when the player moves to the inside of the map but remains the same near the edges. Collect all of the blue and red discs to win, and avoid the purple robots. The purple robots will move around at random, and when the player gets close will chase the player. The red discs will temporarily empower the player, and nearby purple robots will flee. However, the player is faster and can catch and destroy the robots when empowered.
